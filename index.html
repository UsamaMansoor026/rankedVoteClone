how ranked choice voting works

Voters rank once. Strongest support wins.

1
Voters rank ballots

Each voter selects their most preferred choices on the ballot. First, second, third, and so on. In the first round, the top ranked choices are counted to determine if a choice received enough votes to win.

2
Lowest vote getters are eliminated

If no choice gets enough votes to win, the choice with the least votes is eliminated. Those who voted for an eliminated choice have their vote redistributed to their next choice in the following round.

3
Repeat until a winner is found

In the next round, the redistributed votes are added to the remaining choices' totals. The elimination and redistribution process repeats automatically until a choice receives enough votes to win.
